# Finals Retrospective Artifact

### How do I interpret user needs and implement them into a program? How does creating "user stories" help with this?
Interpreting user needs starts with breaking down complex, ambiguous requirements into concrete pieces. In the SNHU Travel project, the Product Owner did exactly this, they translated broader goals into prioritized user stories in the backlog, which gave the development team a clear idea of what needed to be built and in what order. User stories helped by keeping development focused on functionality from the user's perspective. Instead of building the entire application at once, we worked on individual features like booking functionality or user account management.


### How do I approach developing programs? What Agile processes do I hope to incorporate into my future development work?
I approach development iteratively, favoring smaller cycles over trying to solve everything at once. Sprint planning, backlog refinement, and sprint reviews all gave each iteration a clear direction and a built-in checkpoint for evaluating progress. Going forward, I want to carry these practices into future work, especially the ability to handle changing requirements mid project by reprioritizing the backlog rather than disrupting the whole process.


### What does it mean to be a good team member in software development?
Being a good team member means communicating consistently, staying engaged with the team goal, and actively helping remove obstacles for others. As Scrum Master on this project, that looked like clarifying requirements when there was confusion, adjusting priorities to keep the team moving, and fostering an environment where everyone felt comfortable raising concerns.
